About this project

Vruksha began with a simple ache — the feeling that somewhere along the way, we lost touch with where our food comes from. Most of us grew up with a mango tree somewhere in the family: a grandparent's backyard, a village summer, the taste of a fruit picked at the exact right moment and eaten warm from the sun. That tree was never just a product. It was a memory, a season, a part of who we were.

Today, that tree is mostly gone. Mangoes reach us picked too early and ripened with chemicals, stripped of the patience that once made them special. Vruksha is a small, honest attempt to give that back — to let you have a tree of your own again, cared for with love, and to taste a mango the way it was always meant to taste.

This is also a personal beginning. Vruksha is my first real step into building something of my own — not to chase quick profit, but to create genuine trust between the people who grow our food and the people who eat it. It is equal parts terrifying and beautiful, and it feels like a dream I get to build one tree at a time.

Thank you for being here at the very start. You are not just renting a tree — you are helping grow something we hope will outlast all of us.

For farmers

For too long, the people who grow our food have been the last to be paid fairly. A farmer pours an entire year into a single tree — the watering, the waiting, the worry over every storm — and then watches most of the value vanish into the hands of middlemen, traders, and agents before it ever reaches their pocket. The market eats the margin. The farmer is left with scraps.

We believe that is backwards. At Vruksha, our whole purpose is to put the farmer and the family who eats the fruit on the same side — farmer to consumer, directly (F2C). No long chain of brokers. No hidden cuts. No one in the middle quietly taking what was never theirs.

When you rent a tree, the farmer earns a fair, honest price for their care and their craft — often far more than the wholesale market would ever give them. And because we have removed the layers in between, you receive fresher, better produce, usually for less than you would pay in a store. The farmer wins. You win. Only the middleman misses out.

This is the heart of what we are building: a fairer, kinder food chain — one tree, and one family, at a time.

Frequently asked questions

What does it mean to “rent” a mango tree?

You reserve a real, living mango tree on one of our partner farms for the season. Our farmers care for it all year, and you receive its natural harvest, delivered to your home.

How much fruit will I get?

Each plan comes with a guaranteed minimum harvest — at least 10 kg (₹999), 20 kg (₹1,999), or 40 kg (₹3,999). If your tree falls short of its minimum, we make up the difference from our orchard network.

When will I receive my mangoes?

Mangoes are delivered in line with the natural harvest season. Our first deliveries begin from April 2027, and we keep you updated throughout.

How do I reserve a tree?

Simply pay ₹199 to reserve your tree and plan. The balance of your plan is payable before the harvest season begins.

What if I’m not satisfied?

If you are not happy with the produce you receive, we will refund your ₹199 reservation amount back to your account. See our Refund & Cancellation Policy for details.

Are the mangoes ripened with chemicals?

No. Our mangoes are grown naturally and ripened without artificial ripening agents such as calcium carbide.

Where do you deliver?

We currently deliver to major cities across India. When you place an order, we will confirm whether we can reach your location.

How can I pay?

Payments are made securely online. You can pay by UPI, debit or credit card, or net banking.

Can I visit my tree?

Yes! We welcome members to visit the partner farm by prior appointment to see how their tree is grown and cared for.

Can I share a tree with others?

Yes — our co-rent option lets you split a tree and its cost with friends or family, so more people can enjoy a season of fresh mangoes together.
